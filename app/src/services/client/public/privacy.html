Privacy

Definitions


Privacy

The ability to selectively reveal yourself.


Personally Identifiable Information

Data that can be used to identify you, such as your name, ip address, email address, phone number, or other information that can be used to contact you.


End-to-end encryption

The encryption of your data using a key which is shared only with intended recipients; allowing only the recipients to view the data.

Implementation

Cypherpost was built to maximize privacy.

This not only protects users, but also the admin.


Public Data

All data flowing through cypherpost clients and servers are end-to-end encrypted with the exception of:

  • Username
  • Nickname
  • Status
  • Who trusts whom.

The reason for the above mentioned data being in plain-text is to allow users to have some part of their profile public to expand their network by making some information about themselves publicly available.

Private Data

  • Contact Info
  • Posts

Data Analytics

We DO NOT, and have no interest in, collecting or analyzing personally identifiable information.

You have the ability to DELETE your profile in its entirety with no traces left behind.

NOTE: We encourage users to always use either a VPN or Tor to prevent IP tracking. Although we do not actively collect IP information, this cannot be verified and therefore constitutes better usage practice.
