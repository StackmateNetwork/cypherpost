Protocol

cypherpost is an end-to-end encrypted social network protocol to share posts among trusted peers.

It was developed to allow inviduals to post advertisements to trade Bitcoin among trusted peers without the need of an escrow.

The protocol developed to fulfil this need serves as a basis for a portable and private end-to-end encryption model for any social network.

Features


Portable E2EE

Currently, all e2ee applications use RSA keys. The problem with RSA keys are primarily the lack of portability and the need to maintain a master device to recover your account.

cypherpost leverages the flexibility of the BIP32 HD Key standard and simiplicity of BIP39 to achieve a more portable form of e2ee.


Trust Model

The way a user expands their network on cypherpost is through the 'Trust' action.

Trusting another user, gives them access to view your posts and contact information. This give you privacy via the ability to selectively reveal yourself.


Muting Users

If many users trust you, you will have a lot of posts on your feed. You have the ability to 'Mute' users that are flooding your feed with unwanted and unproductive posts.


Mechanism


ECDSA Shared Secret

At the core of the e2ee model is the ECDSA shared secret.

All users have a primary 'recipient' key pair comprised of a public key and private key.

Given two users, Alice and Bob; both can compute the same shared secret with their private key and the other's public key.

This shared secret is used to encrypt the key that is used to encrypt/decrypt cypherdata (posts or contact_info).

If a user is trusting 10 other users, they only encrypt their data with a single key; and then, encrypt this key with a shared secret computed for each of their trusted users (creating 10 copies of encrypted keys) and share the encrypted key with each of them; which they can each use to decrypt on their end and use to view the cypherdata.


Revocation

When you stop trusting a user (revoke trust) you reencrypt your cypherdata with a new key and repeat the process of computing the shared secret and encrypting the key with the shared secret for all your remaining trusted users.


Bitcoin Keys

The BIP32 key derivation model is used to derive an infinite amount of child keys that are used to encrypt/decrypt all data flowing through cypherpost. This is done by deriving a new key from the user's master key which is derived from a BIP39 seed.

We use the following path standard: m/purpose'/service'/usecase'/index'/revoke'; with 3 main use cases:

  • Recipient: Used to compute shared secrets
  • Profile: Used to encrypt/decrypt profile
  • Posts: Used to encrypt/decrypt posts

More information can be found at BIP32InCypherPost
